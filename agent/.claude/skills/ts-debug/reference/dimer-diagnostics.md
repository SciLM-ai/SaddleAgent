## Convergence criterion ‚Äî force AND negative curvature (never "force only")
A min-mode saddle search (dimer, Lanczos) is converged only when **both** hold: the mode-projected
force (OUTCAR `FORCES: max atom`) ‚â§ `|EDIFFG|`, **and** the lowest-mode curvature is **negative**
(DIMCAR `Curvature` < 0). Low force with positive/zero curvature = a minimum or a degenerate (monkey)
saddle, **not** a first-order TS ‚Äî re-seed / break symmetry, don't call it converged. Judge the force
by OUTCAR `FORCES: max atom`, not the mode-projected DIMCAR `Force` column. Confirm the negative mode
at the *start* of any refine (none ‚áí nothing to refine) and re-confirm at the end with a frequency
check (exactly one imaginary mode). (Criteria: `docs/vtst/{dimer,lanczos,optimizers}.md`.)

## TS re-optimization stalls after a k-point / accuracy upgrade ‚Äî finish the re-relax, never substitute a single-point
**Symptom.** A saddle converged at *reduced* k-points (the cheap-search economy ‚Äî drop to 1√ó1√ó1 once forces fall below ~0.1 eV/√Ö) is re-relaxed at the production k-mesh (`IBRION=1`/dimer, small `POTIM` ~0.05‚Äì0.1) and the force **stalls or overshoots** *regardless of `POTIM`* ‚Äî ~0.1‚Äì0.2 eV/√Ö above `|EDIFFG|`; moves *away* from the TS at larger `POTIM`, won't settle at smaller. Tempting shortcut: a single-point at production k-points on the coarse-k TS geometry.
**Why.** Reconverging a *true* coarse-k saddle on a finer mesh almost always *succeeds*, so failure means either the coarse geometry **wasn't a genuine saddle** (no negative eigenvalue) / the dimer lost a well-resolved negative mode (`POTIM` can't substitute for accurate curvature), or denser k-sampling *shifted* the surface so the coarse saddle is no longer stationary and the residual force is *real*, not noise. Either way a single-point can't repair it: the TS geometry must itself meet `EDIFFG` *at the final level of theory*.
**Fix ‚Äî diagnose, don't bypass.** **Confirm a negative eigenvalue at the start** (OUTCAR / DIMCAR `Curvature`) ‚Äî none ‚áí nothing to refine. Then (a) tighten `EDIFFG` at the coarse mesh *before* upgrading so the start sits nearer the finer-mesh saddle; (b) a short dimer/quasi-Newton refine at full k-points from the coarse TS (mode known ‚áí cheap), **raising `DRotMax`** so it's well-resolved before each translation (POTIM secondary to curvature); (c) keep `POTIM` from oscillating but not *over*-timid ‚Äî a modestly larger `POTIM` won't slide a *true* saddle into IS/FS while the negative mode stays correct (frequency-check if unsure). A stall surviving all this is a real signal (shifted/under-resolved saddle), not licence to shortcut.
**Rule.** Upgrading k-points (or any accuracy DOF) for a converged TS = *re-relax* to `EDIFFG` at production level, never a single-point. It should *succeed* if the coarse geometry was a true saddle ‚Äî confirm a genuine negative mode, raise `DRotMax` to resolve it (POTIM secondary), tighten EDIFFG before upgrading; don't dodge it. (Reconverge-at-full-precision step of the cheap-search economy ‚Äî torque entry below / workflows.md.) (Expert-stated 2018; level-of-theory consistency not version-specific.)

## Spin-polarized TM dimer/NEB won't converge ‚Äî forces oscillate/spike, curvature erratic ‚Äî the spin state is changing along the path (electronic, not geometric)
**Symptom.** On a transition-metal system a spin-polarized dimer (or NEB) won't reach EDIFFG: forces oscillate/spike instead of dropping monotonically, DIMCAR `Curvature` goes erratic (or positive) at high force, and the TM atom's magnetic moment flips between dimer steps / NEB images. Geometry is sane.
**Why.** Electronic hysteresis ‚Äî the system falls into a *different spin state* at different geometries along the reaction coordinate, so the PES is discontinuous and the optimizer is fighting an electronic local minimum, not geometry. (Same root as the NEB "images on inconsistent magnetic/electronic states" entry, neb-diagnostics.md; here it surfaces as step-to-step instability within one dimer.) Distinct from deck force-noise (torque entry below): there the spin state is *steady*, here it *changes*.
**Diagnose ‚Äî ISPIN=1 first.** Re-run with `ISPIN=1` to decouple electronic from geometric convergence: if forces then drop cleanly, the geometry was fine and the spin flip was the bottleneck. `grep magnetization OUTCAR` (or `*/OUTCAR`) ‚Äî moment off the smooth trend where the force spikes confirms it.
**Fix.** Re-introduce spin with the state *locked*: converge a single-point at the target spin state and start the dimer/NEB from that density (seed `WAVECAR`/`CHGCAR`), or use occupation-matrix control (LDAUTYPE occupation-matrix seeding) to hold the d-occupation; with explicit `MAGMOM` converged to the lowest-energy state on all TM atoms (NEB entry for the per-image handwork).
**Rule.** A spin-polarized TM dimer/NEB that won't converge with oscillating/spiking forces and erratic curvature is usually the *spin state changing along the path*, not geometry ‚Äî `ISPIN=1` isolates it immediately; then lock the spin (converge-to-target-state first, or occupation-matrix control) before the production run. (Expert-stated 2018; electronic-hysteresis behavior not version-specific.)

## Dimer won't lock onto a high-symmetry saddle (bridge / 3-fold site) ‚Äî it's a monkey saddle, not a first-order TS
**Symptom.** NEB puts the saddle on a high-symmetry site (bridge, or a top site with 3-fold neighbors); the dimer then runs long without converging, and when it finally breaks symmetry it relaxes to a nearby *lower*-symmetry site. Several equivalent downhill paths are visible from the saddle geometry.
**Why.** The symmetric point is a *monkey saddle* (3-fold-or-higher symmetry, multiple equivalent descent channels), not a true first-order saddle ‚Äî it isn't a single negative-curvature point, so the dimer oscillates between degenerate exit channels and can't lock onto one.
**Check.** Inspect the symmetry of the highest-energy NEB image CONTCAR. Confirm saddle order with a **frequency/Hessian calc before seeding the dimer**: a first-order TS has exactly one imaginary mode along the reaction coordinate; a monkey saddle shows more, or no single channel.
**Fix.** Seed the dimer from a **symmetry-broken POSCAR** ‚Äî manually displace along *one* equivalent channel ‚Äî so it converges to one first-order saddle; cross-check the barrier with `ISPIN=1` to fix the geometry before adding spin complexity.
**Rule.** A high-symmetry site (bridge, 3-fold top) may be a monkey saddle with several equivalent descent paths, not a first-order TS ‚Äî confirm saddle order by frequency before blaming dimer convergence, and seed a symmetry-broken structure to force one downhill channel. (Expert-stated 2018; symmetry argument not version-specific.)

## Dimer on a both-face symmetric (dipole-cancellation) slab ‚Äî a second-order saddle; `ISYM=0` decouples the faces anyway
**Symptom.** A slab with the *same* adsorbate on **both** faces (mirror symmetry, to zero the slab dipole) is handed to a dimer; the user asks which face's saddle to keep, or the two faces end up at *different* geometries by the end.
**Why.** A dimer mandates `ISYM=0` (parameters.md), killing the symmetry the both-face design leaned on, so the faces relax independently ‚Äî and the intended state (*both* adsorbates at a saddle **simultaneously**) is a **second-order saddle**, outside a dimer's one-negative-mode reach (first-order only; non-monotonic entry below). Same root as the NEB barrier-doubling / symmetry-breaking case (neb-diagnostics.md symmetric-slab entry).
**Fix.** Drop the mirror pair ‚Äî model **one** adsorbate on a single face and cancel the dipole with VASP's correction (`LDIPOL=.TRUE.`, `IDIPOL=3` for a z-slab); it needs no symmetric slab. (Freezing one adsorbate in a minimum and saddling the other works mechanically but defeats the dipole-cancellation purpose.)
**Rule.** A both-face dipole-cancellation slab encodes a second-order saddle a dimer can't find, and its mandatory `ISYM=0` decouples the faces regardless ‚Äî use a single adsorbate + dipole corrections instead. (Expert-stated 2007; method-scope/dipole argument not version-specific.)

## Dimer silently runs like a plain relaxation ‚Äî the binary lacks the VTST code (no `VTST` line in OUTCAR)
**Symptom.** A dimer launches and runs without error but behaves like an *ordinary* VASP ionic relaxation ‚Äî no min-mode/rotation, geometry just relaxes downhill ‚Äî though `ICHAIN=2` (and the dimer `IBRION`/`IOPT` tags) are set, **and no DIMCAR (or other VTST output file) is written** ‚Äî the missing VTST files are themselves the cheapest tell. Often the *NEB* on the same system worked, making it puzzling why the dimer ignores its method.
**Why.** The dimer was run with a VASP binary that does **not have the VTST code linked in**. Without VTST the dimer/Lanczos tags are *silently ignored* (VASP doesn't error on unrecognized tags ‚Äî cf. the misspelled-tag entry, parameters.md). Classic cause: a *different* binary was picked up for the dimer than for the NEB ‚Äî the NEB binary was VTST-linked, the dimer one wasn't (different `$PATH`, module, or hardcoded path in the job script).
**Check.** `grep VTST OUTCAR` ‚Äî a `VTST: version ‚Ä¶` line present ‚áí VTST linked; **absent ‚áí not linked**, so every method-specific tag was ignored (and no DIMCAR appears). Compare against the working NEB OUTCAR to confirm a different binary.
**Fix.** Rerun the dimer with the **same VTST-linked binary** used for the NEB; confirm the `VTST: version` line before trusting *any* dimer/Lanczos/frequency result.
**Rule.** A VTST method that silently acts like plain VASP = wrong (non-VTST) binary, not a tag/setup bug; `grep VTST OUTCAR` for a version line is the first check, and use one VTST-linked binary for every VTST method (NEB, dimer, Lanczos, freq). (Expert-stated VASP 5.4.4 / VTST 3.2, 2020; silent-ignore + linkage behavior not version-specific.)

## End of a dimer / Lanczos run ‚Äî the converged saddle is CONTCAR, not CENTCAR and not the per-step `lanczos.out`/DIMCAR log (take the barrier energy from the final OUTCAR)
**Symptom.** At the end of a dimer run CENTCAR and CONTCAR differ and **CENTCAR's `FORCES: max atom` is *larger* than CONTCAR's** (CONTCAR meets `|EDIFFG|`, CENTCAR doesn't). The user is unsure which is the converged TS, and whether to read the barrier energy from the last image or from the line above the last `Dimer: Central Point`. Or, more basically: a Lanczos (or dimer) user opens only the run's *log* ‚Äî `lanczos.out` / DIMCAR ‚Äî finds just the **initial-guess** coordinates and per-step numbers, and concludes the search produced no saddle, never having checked CONTCAR/OUTCAR.
**Why.** CENTCAR is written *only* at the `Dimer: Central Point` task ‚Äî the start of each dimer iteration, holding the center R0. Each iteration then does trial/rotation steps and finally an **OPT translation step that moves the center**, and the force-convergence check (`F < |EDIFFG|`) is applied *after* that translation. So at termination the final translation has stepped *past* the last-written CENTCAR: **CONTCAR holds the converged, post-translation geometry; CENTCAR is one dimer iteration stale** (hence its higher force). CONTCAR is also consistent with CHG*/WAV* (written every ionic step); CENTCAR is not.
**Check.** `grep -nE 'Dimer: Central Point|FORCES: max atom' OUTCAR` ‚Äî the final `FORCES` line (a translation/OPT step, *below* `|EDIFFG|`) sits *after* the last `Central Point`; that translation, not the central point, is the converged geometry. CONTCAR's force < CENTCAR's confirms CENTCAR lags.
**Fix.** Use **CONTCAR** as the authoritative saddle geometry ‚Äî for continuation, TS confirmation, or frequency input ‚Äî and reuse CHG*/WAV* with it (they match). For the **barrier energy take the final OUTCAR `energy(sigma->0)`** (the converged translation), *not* the energy above the last `Dimer: Central Point` (that's the stale CENTCAR geometry).
**Restart after a mid-step (wall-time) kill ‚Äî OLD builds only.** Same CENTCAR-is-always-the-center fact behind the expert's *2008/2010* rule to restart a killed dimer from (or do downstream freq/SP on) **CENTCAR**, not CONTCAR: old vtstcode wrote the *last-evaluated* position to CONTCAR, which mid-iteration is a *displaced* image (center ¬± NdR), so copying it to POSCAR flips **curvature negative‚Üípositive and jumps forces ~10√ó** on restart (the telltale ‚Äî *not* a bad Hessian guess or wrong optimizer; switching IOPT 1‚Üî2 won't fix it). Modern builds write the *center* to CONTCAR every ionic step, so plain `CONTCAR‚ÜíPOSCAR` continuation is fine; only if you see that flip-on-restart symptom on an old build, copy **CENTCAR** ‚Äî or run `vfin.pl`, which substitutes CENTCAR automatically, so its restart POSCAR matching *neither* the original POSCAR nor CONTCAR is **expected, not a bug**.
**Rule.** At dimer convergence CONTCAR is the best saddle estimate (consistent with CHG*/WAV*); CENTCAR lags one dimer iteration. Geometry‚ÜíCONTCAR, energy‚Üífinal OUTCAR, and the search file (`lanczos.out`/DIMCAR) is *only* a per-step convergence log (opened alone it shows just the initial guess) ‚Äî true for **dimer and Lanczos** alike, a stable VASP/VTST output convention (longstanding since ‚â•2006, method-independent). Some builds add a `Dimer_Fin` source patch that writes CENTCAR at convergence detection ‚Äî then CENTCAR matches too, but CONTCAR is safe either way (that patch is version-specific: grep the subroutine, don't trust quoted line numbers). (Expert *corrected his earlier CENTCAR-is-the-saddle / restart-from-CENTCAR advice*: stated 2008‚Äì2010, corrected 2021-08-11 on VASP 5.4.x / vtstcode-184; current builds write the center to CONTCAR each ionic step, so CONTCAR supersedes for both end-of-run saddle ID and continuation.)

## Dimer `Force` *or total energy* non-monotonic ‚Äî live health is **negative curvature**, but convergence still needs force < `|EDIFFG|` (negative curvature ‚âÝ converged); never judge by the force *or energy* trend, don't reflex-tune IOPT/POTIM/EDIFFG/rotation-count
**Symptom.** DIMCAR `Force` climbs/oscillates, *or* drops steadily toward `|EDIFFG|` but isn't there yet, while `Curvature` stays clearly negative; **or** `vef.pl`/OUTCAR per-step forces jump sharply at a *regular interval* (e.g. every ~4 ionic steps) then recover, the envelope still trending down; **or** OUTCAR `TOTEN` *falls* (or wanders) and the user ‚Äî expecting the saddle to be the energy *maximum* ‚Äî calls the run wrong. User weighs aborting/restarting, asks whether to switch `IOPT`/bump `POTIM` ‚Äî **or** (the mirror error) sees curvature consistently negative and calls it **converged** while max force is still above `|EDIFFG|` (e.g. 0.10 vs 0.03) ‚Äî **or** compares the DIMCAR per-step columns to a tutorial/reference example, sees different numbers, and fears the run is wrong.
**Why.** A dimer follows the lowest mode ‚Äî *maximizing* energy along the one negative-curvature direction while *minimizing* along all others; that geometry (a max along exactly one mode, a min along every perpendicular one) *is* the definition of a first-order saddle, so a converged min-mode search lands on a saddle **by construction** ‚Äî never a minimum (a minimum has no negative mode to climb out along). Climb the single most-negative mode: maximizing along *any* mode reaches a first-order saddle only if that mode is also the lowest at convergence (the dimer ensures this by following the lowest from the start) ‚Äî try to maximize the *2nd*-lowest instead and it never converges, the two modes **exchange identity** mid-search. That single-mode climb also *bounds* what a dimer can reach ‚Äî **only a first-order saddle**; a **2nd-order saddle needs two modes maximized at once**, which it can't do, so to find one (or the path *between two known first-order TSs*) run **CI-NEB between those TSs** ‚Äî even a coarse few-image band exposes the connectivity and the higher saddle's approximate location, cleanest on a symmetric path. Hence **neither force nor energy is monotonic** en route: the energy often *decreases* (perpendicular relaxation outweighs the uphill step) because the saddle is a gradient stationary point, **not** a global energy max. In a **`vef.pl`/OUTCAR per-step trace** that non-monotonicity is *periodic* ‚Äî a sharp force jump every few ionic steps ‚Äî because each translation is preceded by the dimer's **off-minimum probe evaluations**: center force, lowest-mode displacement, finite-difference rotation, and (under `IOPT=2`/CG) a line-optimization FD step, so one cycle ‚âà 2¬∑(rotation sub-steps) + 1 ‚âà 3‚Äì4 force evals (center + 2/rotation + a translation/line-min FD step) ‚Äî the bundle **grows with the rotation count** (more rotations per translation, e.g. legacy FD-rotation `DMaxNR`>1, ‚áí a wider OSZICAR‚ÜîDIMCAR gap); the displacement+rotation pair is dimer-specific and is *why a dimer converges slower than a plain minimization*. This is also why **DIMCAR step N ‚âÝ OSZICAR ionic-step N**: every force eval is a VASP ionic step, so N dimer translations ‚áí ‚âà(force-evals/step)¬∑N + 1 final OSZICAR entries (4¬∑6+1 = 25 for 6 steps) ‚Äî *neither count is wrong* (DIMCAR tracks dimer convergence; OSZICAR/XDATCAR log every force call), so to see only the significant moves **filter XDATCAR to the N DIMCAR-step frames**. Read the **envelope of the translation (center) steps**, not the raw per-step values ‚Äî the spike period = force-evals/cycle, which analytic-rotation (modern Heyden) builds change. Negative curvature with force above `|EDIFFG|` isn't success ‚Äî it's **necessary, not sufficient** ("converging, not converged"). (`POTIM` is a no-op under `IOPT>0` ‚Äî VTST steps via `TIMESTEP`.)
**Read it / abort.** Judge live health by `Curvature` sign, never the force *or energy* trend ‚Äî negative ‚áí keep running; a **sustained** flip to positive ‚áí never near a saddle, the real abort signal (curvature-drift entry below) ‚Äî **a high *initial* torque *and* large `Angle` (tens of degrees) with force not yet dropping ‚Äî typical of a *random/misaligned MODECAR* ‚Äî is fine the instant a *strong negative curvature appears in step 1*: that step-1 negative mode is the tell the start sits in a saddle basin; the dimer then climbs it (rotation refining the mode) while relaxing the rest, so force *lags*, dropping only once the mode aligns. Same self-correction as a *transient* one-step torque/curvature spike (brief sign flip, torque ‚â´10) recovering to persistently negative curvature with energy still dropping ‚Äî rotating off a *ridge the start grazed*: keep running, convergence accelerates once the negative mode steadies. Re-seed from a better MODECAR only if negative curvature isn't back within ~5 ionic steps.** True convergence = OUTCAR `FORCES: max atom` < `|EDIFFG|` (one-step/DIMCAR-Force entry above), not DIMCAR points, curvature, nor energy: force above `|EDIFFG|` with negative curvature ‚áí **converging, not converged ‚Äî continue**, no restart or parameter change. Rotation and translation converge *separately* and translation **lags**: **declining torque/`Angle` mid-run = orientation settling, after which translational force convergence typically *accelerates*** ‚Äî a still-high force while rotation is settling is the *least* reason to abort. A residual rotation `Angle` of 1‚Äì2¬∞ *at* force convergence is likewise normal ‚Äî accept the saddle (force < `|EDIFFG|`, curvature negative), don't abort because the `Angle` column never reached zero. Over-rotation (many rotation sub-steps per translation, small `Angle`) ‚áí **lower `DRotMax`** toward default **4** (‚â≤2‚Äì3 if well-behaved: torque ‚â≤2, `Angle`‚â™`DRotMax`, mode stable); raise only to resolve a hard mode on a *persistent* stall, never to add rotations for one noisy step while the force still trends down (rotation-torque entry below). **Per-step DIMCAR columns aren't bit-for-bit reproducible** across platforms/MPI task counts (reduction order, FP, slightly different initial dimer orientation) ‚Äî they shift without moving the converged saddle, so *matching a tutorial/reference run is no test either*; judge only your own run by the criteria above. (Expert-stated 2007/2008/2009/2011/2014/2018/2022; not version-specific.)

## Dimer drifts / won't converge ‚Äî OR converges cleanly to a *different* nearby saddle than intended ‚Äî the start wasn't near the *target* saddle
**Symptom.** A from-scratch dimer either (a) **won't reach EDIFFG** ‚Äî forces stay high: CONTCAR drifting far from the starting POSCAR (large cumulative translation, "searching" the PES), *or* (the mirror) forces *extreme* (hundreds of eV/√Ö) from step 1 while the structure barely evolves (CONTCAR‚âàPOSCAR, the intermediate "looks like IS/FS") and the user reflexively blames a tolerance (`SdR`/`FDSTEP`/`SLTOL`); or (b) **converges** (low force, looks done) but to a real saddle for a *different* process ‚Äî seeded for a bond dissociation it lands on a surface-diffusion hop, so CONTCAR sits on an unrelated saddle (or back near the starting minimum), not the intended TS ‚Äî often recurring across analogous reactions set up the same way.
**Why.** The dimer is a *local* min-mode optimizer, not a global finder: from a minimum it climbs along the general MODECAR direction to *whichever* nearby saddle lies that way ‚Äî not necessarily the target reaction's. In a basin / far up a wall it drifts off (no nearby saddle); near a *different* low-barrier saddle it converges there. A MODECAR built from IS/FS endpoints is only a rough proxy for the reaction coordinate, so it can aim at the wrong process. It is built for an **unknown product** ‚Äî a reaction whose two endpoints you *already* have is the wrong problem for it. A separate root for the extreme-force/stuck variant: an **unphysical short contact** in the start (a bond far below equilibrium ‚Äî e.g. O‚Äìmetal ~1 √Ö where ~2 √Ö is normal) generates forces of hundreds of eV/√Ö that swamp the search direction, so the geometry can't evolve toward the saddle ‚Äî a *geometry* error, not a tolerance one.
**Check.** Diff CONTCAR vs the initial POSCAR (large drift) **and vs the intended product** ‚Äî converged-but-wrong-saddle ‚áí CONTCAR matches neither (sits on an unrelated site); `grep 'FORCES: max atom' OUTCAR` (not DIMCAR) for the real convergence state. For the extreme-force variant, read **step-1** `FORCES: max atom` (hundreds of eV/√Ö ‚áí red flag) and scan POSCAR/CONTCAR bond lengths for a short contact ‚Äî fix that before any tolerance.
**Fix.** First, when forces are extreme, **fix the starting geometry** ‚Äî relax or manually correct the short contact to a physical bond length ‚Äî before touching tolerances or method. Then decide it's the right method. **Know *both* reactant and product ‚áí use NEB to *find* the saddle, not a from-scratch dimer** ‚Äî NEB interpolates the known endpoints to the connecting saddle; the dimer only *complements* it, cheaply *reconverging* the NEB-located saddle (rough-NEB max image ‚Üí `neb2dim.pl`, or `neb2lan.pl` for Lanczos ‚Üí refined MODECAR from adjacent images), ideal for convergence tests at *different* settings (workflows.md). Even a 1‚Äì3-image NEB to a rough but physical path before `neb2dim.pl`/`neb2lan.pl` sharply cuts saddle-search steps. **Product unknown / only a rough TS region ‚áí** it needs a better start: a NEB/CI-NEB max-energy image, an IRC point, or an analogous known TS, with a MODECAR along the reaction coordinate (verify direction ‚Äî MODECAR entry). **For an *unanticipated* or *alternative* mechanism** ‚Äî product unknown so CI-NEB isn't an option, *or* the dimer keeps re-finding the *same* saddle while changing the MODECAR/target final state does nothing (it's **initial-configuration-driven, not final-state-driven**: once the start lies in a saddle's basin it re-finds *that* saddle) ‚Äî **vary the *starting POSCAR*, not the mode/target**: **displace *undercoordinated/active* atoms (strained bonds) at an *intermediate* magnitude** (or random-displace the minimum, or take NEB images still *far* from the TS) and follow the lowest-curvature mode to whatever saddle exists ‚Äî too *small* a kick stays in the harmonic well (step-1 curvature near-zero/positive ‚Äî only soft, non-reactive modes), too *large* lands on saddles disconnected from the initial minimum; tune it so the **first dimer step already shows a negative mode**, never restart from an NEB-refined near-saddle structure when hunting other pathways, and accept some failed searches as the cost of broad coverage.
**Also watch ‚Äî read the curvature.** Flipping negative‚Üípositive *within a rotation step* = force noise ‚Äî tighten `EDIFF=1e-8` (parameters.md). Flipping *across ionic steps* = left the intended mode: a shoulder/no saddle (next entry) or an under-converged NEB seed migrating to a *different* saddle ‚Äî re-seed from a tighter NEB (‚â≤0.01 eV/√Ö), don't rotate-tune. (Only a start *near* the target that merely *oversteps* ‚Äî large per-step translation, CONTCAR drifting ‚Äî is fixable by a knob: shorten the translational step, lower `MAXMOVE`, and raise `DRotMax` to resolve the mode before each translation; an off-target start, no knob saves.) **Near zero (not negative) with all atoms drifting by one shared vector** = riding a **translational (rigid-body) zero mode** ‚Äî a whole-cell MODECAR, or a POSCAR far from any saddle, so the lowest mode found is the translation (`grep 'Dimer: CN' OUTCAR` / DIMCAR curvature col ‚âà 0; movie shows uniform drift, relative positions fixed). The dimer only searches on *negative* curvature ‚Äî fix the *initialization*, not convergence: start near the saddle, displace a *single atom* in MODECAR (not a cell-wide vector), or **free one atom** (`F F F` the rest) to mechanically suppress rigid translation.
**Rule.** A from-scratch dimer drifts off, converges to a *different* nearby saddle, or stalls with extreme step-1 forces ‚Äî either the start wasn't near the *target* saddle (MODECAR only roughly aims at the reaction coordinate) or it's *unphysical* (short contact ‚áí sanity-check bond lengths and fix the geometry, never tune `SdR`/`FDSTEP`/`SLTOL`). With *both* endpoints let NEB *find* it (even 1‚Äì3 images), then dimer *complements* by reconverging it (rough NEB ‚Üí `neb2dim.pl`/`neb2lan.pl`); reserve a from-scratch dimer for an unknown/unanticipated product. To reach an *alternative* saddle, vary the starting POSCAR (start far from any known TS), never the MODECAR or target final state ‚Äî the dimer is initial-config-driven. Diagnose by comparing CONTCAR to the intended product and inspecting bond lengths, not just force/drift. (Expert-stated, incl. 2007, 2010 & 2012; geometry-sanity, method selection, local-min-mode/initial-config-driven & exploratory-displacement behavior not version-specific.)

## Dimer "converges" then shoots off ‚Äî a PES shoulder (true barrierless, or a too-loose NEB handoff)
**Symptom.** The dimer looks like it's homing in (curvature negative, force dropping) for a few ionic steps, then the **curvature flips negative‚Üípositive across ionic steps and the force diverges** ‚Äî it shoots off / migrates to a different region; force never meets EDIFFG and tightening `DRotMax`/`EDIFF`/`DFNMin`/`DFNMax` doesn't help. Often right after a CI-NEB handoff. A CI-NEB on the *same* reaction may also **stall/oscillate in a low-force plateau** with no convergence trend.
**Why.** On a *shoulder* the energy is flat from reactant toward the expected TS: a negative mode exists but **disappears when followed** ‚Äî the dimer senses it, starts down, loses it, climbs away. Two cases: **(a) no first-order saddle** ‚Äî the PES drops straight to product, barrierless (common for a single light-atom transfer, e.g. H-transfer); a CI-NEB on the same reaction *wanders a low-force plateau* with no trend. **(b) the saddle exists but the CI-NEB pre-resolved the ridge too poorly** ‚Äî pre-converged too loosely (~0.05 eV/√Ö), *or run with too few images for a long-displacement path* (many atoms move but the TS is *localized*, e.g. a single-atom transfer, so the coarse band straddles the true peak with no node on it), leaving the handoff *beside* the ridge. (Drift *alone*, no CI-NEB context, can instead be a bad initial guess ‚Äî entry above.)
**Definitive check (cheapest screen for *any* failed saddle search).** Relax the **reactant** tightly (`IBRION=2`/LBFGS, `EDIFF‚â§1e-6`, `EDIFFG‚â§-0.01`): slides *straight to product* ‚áí never a separate minimum ‚áí (a) barrierless, method **not** at fault; settles into a genuine minimum ‚áí (b) real barrier. (Force dips then rises as energy falls to product = the no-saddle signature.)
**Fix.** **(a)** Stop ‚Äî the reaction is barrierless; re-examine the mechanism if a barrier was expected (wrong product, missing co-adsorbate, different coordinate). **(b)** *Get closer with the NEB* ‚Äî tighten pre-convergence (smaller `|EDIFFG|`, more `NSW`) **and, for a long-displacement path, add images** (e.g. ‚â•8) so a band node lands on the true ridge before seeding; then re-hand-off only once that peak image is sharp and well-converged. Don't retune dimer rotation tolerances (`DFNMin`/`DFNMax`/`DRotMax`) ‚Äî not the cause.
**Rule.** Dimer "converges then shoots off" (curvature flips negative‚Üípositive, force diverges) ‚áí a shoulder ‚Äî the negative mode disappears when followed. Relax the reactant to decide which: slides to product = barrierless, stop; a real minimum = under-resolved NEB handoff, so get closer with the NEB (tighter pre-convergence, **and more images for a long-displacement path**), don't retune `DFNMin`/`DFNMax`/`DRotMax`. (Confirmed 2021; image-count sub-cause confirmed 2017; not version-specific.)

## Dimer torque is never a convergence criterion ‚Äî per-step non-convergence/fluctuation is by design; stuck high & unresponsive to `DRotMax` = deck force noise (not rotation, not `ZHEGV`)
**Symptom.** The rotation minimization hits the max rotation steps every translation step without converging, *or* the DIMCAR `Torque` column just fluctuates between sub-steps instead of decreasing (it "never settles") ‚Äî reads like broken rotation. **Or** torque stays high (~2‚Äì16 eV/√Ö) for many steps with no downward trend and changing `DRotMax` (e.g. 5 vs 50) makes **no difference** (force may even rise after rotation) while **curvature is still negative**: the dimer isn't lost, it won't settle. Common on spin-polarized / DFT+U surfaces.
**Why.** VTST deliberately does *not* force full rotation convergence per translation step ‚Äî exactly resolving the mode far from the saddle is wasteful, so it caps rotation (`DRotMax`) and translates anyway: per-step torque is non-monotone **by design, not a convergence criterion**. (The rotation *angle* is found **analytically** ‚Äî Heyden modified-dimer (fixed-angle trial ‚Üí closed-form optimum), not finite differences ‚Äî so there's **no `delta_theta`/rotation-FD tag to tune**; the only rotation knobs are `DRotMax` (step cap) and `DFNMin`/`DFNMax` (rotational-force stop tolerances).) But if `DRotMax` doesn't move a *persistently high* torque, the limit isn't rotation resolution ‚Äî it's **force noise** swamping the small force difference rotation reads (a finite difference over the dimer separation `DdR`‚âà0.005 √Ö ‚Äî `DdR` is the FD step, *not* a rotation knob, so changing it won't settle a high/stuck torque; parameters.md). Sources: `ALGO=Very_Fast`/`IALGO=48` (vs quieter `IALGO=38`), `EDIFF` coarser than `1e-8`, unfrozen deep layers, over-dense k-mesh / over-hard pseudos, real-space projection (`LREAL=Auto`/`.TRUE.` ‚Äî its *approximate* projectors are noisier than exact reciprocal-space `.FALSE.`, worst on small cells) (parameters.md). **Likely *not* the cause: repeated `EDDRMM: call to ZHEGV failed`** ‚Äî a LAPACK hiccup on DFT+U TM systems; if SCF still *converges* past them they're a red herring (an `ALGO` bump clears the warnings but won't settle the torque). (Distinct from curvature *flipping sign*/drift, below.)
**Check.** `grep -E 'FORCES: max atom|Curvature' OUTCAR` for the real state; `grep Torque` for the rotation trend only. Persistent noise: torque >2 eV/√Ö for 5+ steps with no trend **and** unchanged when you vary `DRotMax` ‚áí `grep -E 'ALGO|IALGO|EDIFF|LREAL' INCAR` (want `IALGO=38`, `EDIFF=1e-8`, `LREAL=.FALSE.`), KPOINTS (Œì-only suffices for a large supercell), POSCAR selective-dynamics (bottom layers `F F F`); `grep 'ZHEGV failed' OUTCAR` only to confirm the warnings are SCF-recoverable (benign).
**Fix.** Per-step fluctuation/non-convergence ‚áí **do nothing** ‚Äî judge by low force (OUTCAR `FORCES: max atom` < `|EDIFFG|`, not the projected DIMCAR Force) + negative curvature, never torque; raise `DRotMax` (e.g. 10) **only near a saddle** (post-CI-NEB handoff) where the mode must be well-resolved before translating ‚Äî far from it extra rotations are wasted. Persistent-noise case ‚áí **debug cheap, reconverge high**: drop precision (`IALGO=38`, `EDIFF=1e-8`, freeze bottom layers, Œì-only, soft pseudos at commensurate `ENCUT`) ‚Äî **and set `LREAL=.FALSE.`**, the one *raise*-accuracy move here (the real-space approximation itself injects the noise, so use exact reciprocal-space projection; under `LREAL=Auto` sub-meV/√Ö forces are unreachable ‚áí either go `.FALSE.` to converge tight, or accept a ~0.01 eV/√Ö force floor) ‚Äî may only *partially* lower the torque (a couple eV/√Ö can survive), fine: the cheap run debugs (clean forces ‚áí noise was the deck) **and** finds the saddle fast; then **reconverge that saddle at full precision** ‚Äî the two accuracy levels also give a barrier **uncertainty estimate**. Don't chase the torque by *tightening* ENCUT/pseudos/k-mesh before the saddle is found (convergence-economy ‚Äî workflows.md).
**Rule.** Torque drives rotation, never convergence ‚Äî judge by low force + negative curvature. Per-step non-convergence/fluctuation is by design (do nothing; raise `DRotMax`‚Üí10 only near a saddle). Torque stuck >2 eV/√Ö unresponsive to `DRotMax` (curvature still negative) = deck force noise (incl. real-space `LREAL=Auto` projection ‚áí `LREAL=.FALSE.`) ‚Äî not rotation, not co-occurring `EDDRMM`/`ZHEGV` (benign if SCF converges); find the saddle cheap, reconverge at full precision (two accuracies = barrier uncertainty). (Expert-stated 2007/2012, 2019, 2021‚Äì2022; analytical-rotation/no-`delta_theta` & LREAL real-space force-noise present VASP 4.x‚Äì6.x, not version-specific.)

## Dimer/Lanczos *crashes* with a LAPACK error (`DSYGV ... not positive definite`, or a parallel-only Lanczos death) ‚Äî the math library / electronic solver, not the VTST code
**Symptom.** A dimer *terminates* (fatal, not a stall) on a linear-algebra crash ‚Äî `DSYGV : ... The matrix (ARG NO. 4) is not positive definite / The leading minor of order (N) has a nonpositive determinant` ‚Äî often only after many RMM SCF steps have run. Deck has an aggressive solver (`IALGO=48` RMM-DIIS / `ALGO=Very_Fast`); the user blames the dimer tags.
**Why.** The crash is in VASP's *core electronic loop* ‚Äî an ill-conditioned overlap/subspace matrix the aggressive solver can't factor ‚Äî **not** the VTST code: dimer tags are a red herring. *Fatal*, unlike the SCF-recoverable `EDDRMM/ZHEGV failed` warnings of the torque-noise entry above.
**Bisect to prove it (cheapest, definitive).** Comment `ICHAIN` **and** `IOPT`, rerun: the *same* crash with the dimer tags gone ‚áí electronic, confirmed ‚Äî nothing to do with VTST. (The general reflex for any crash/odd error "inside" a VTST run: strip the method tags; if plain VASP still breaks, it isn't the VTST code.)
**Fix.** Drop to a less aggressive, more stable solver ‚Äî blocked-Davidson `IALGO=38` (or `ALGO=Normal`/`Fast`) instead of RMM-DIIS `IALGO=48` ‚Äî then restore `ICHAIN`/`IOPT` and continue. Leave the rotation/translation tags alone.
**Parallel-only Lanczos crash, NEB fine ‚áí the LAPACK *build*, not MPI (OLD).** The LAPACK fault can instead sit in the **min-mode eigensolver**: parallel Lanczos/dimer **dies right after step 1** (MPI error or silent exit) while **serial Lanczos *and* parallel NEB/CI-NEB run cleanly on the same binary and cluster**. NEB working **rules out MPI/the cluster** ‚Äî NEB and Lanczos take *different* code paths and only Lanczos hits the sensitive eigensystem LAPACK call ‚Äî so suspect the **BLAS/LAPACK the binary links against**, not the dimer tags: rebuild/relink against a *different* implementation and retry (the era's swap was Intel MKL ‚Üî AMD ACML on 64-bit Opteron). (OLD 2006; the specific MKL/ACML-on-Opteron bug is obsolete, but the reflex ‚Äî *NEB runs while Lanczos crashes ‚áí math library, not MPI* ‚Äî stands.)
**Rule.** A fatal `DSYGV`/non-positive-definite (or similar LAPACK) crash during a dimer = the electronic solver, not a VTST bug: bisect by commenting `ICHAIN`/`IOPT` (reproduces ‚áí electronic), then `IALGO=48`‚Üí`38`. A **parallel-only Lanczos death with NEB fine** is the *library* variant ‚Äî suspect the linked BLAS/LAPACK (relink), not MPI. Either way don't touch the VTST tags. (Expert-stated 2012 vasp.5.2.11 Œ≥-only [DSYGV] & 2006 [parallel-Lanczos/library]; solver-instability behavior not version-specific.)

## Dimer stops after one ionic step, or prints `Force` above `|EDIFFG|` while OUTCAR says converged ‚Äî judge by OUTCAR `FORCES: max atom`, never the printed/DIMCAR `Force`
**Symptom.** A dimer looks prematurely done. (a) **standalone** run whose OUTCAR says `reached required accuracy` while the printed/DIMCAR `Force` stays high (often *identical* across the last sub-steps); (b) **from a converged CI-NEB**, stops after one step, geometry barely moving; (c) the **cheapest tell** ‚Äî DIMCAR is a *single row* with `---` in Torque/Curvature/Angle and `Energy` = the **initial POSCAR** energy: the start force was *already* below `|EDIFFG|`, so it exited *before any rotation* (no curvature ‚áí nothing confirmed a saddle). **(d)** the *genuine* stop, mirror of (a)‚Äì(c): OUTCAR `FORCES: max atom` is *itself* large and the run wrote **no further ionic steps and did not crash** (`lanczos.out`/DIMCAR = one entry, forces still big) ‚Äî it really halted after step 1. (vs entry above: there the per-atom max genuinely sits *above* `|EDIFFG|` ‚áí not converged, continue.)
**Why.** The printed stdout `Force` is an *aggregate* ‚Äî total `|F|` over *all* atoms, several√ó the per-atom max on a big cell; the **DIMCAR `Force` is the total force at the dimer *center***, mode-*projected*, so it differs slightly from *any* single OUTCAR force even at the same step (different quantity, not an error) ‚Äî either way a converged run still shows a big, even *constant*, number. Convergence uses the per-atom max: OUTCAR `FORCES: max atom, RMS` = the **2nd ("by dimension") column** of `FORCE total and by dimension`. (Step counters differ harmlessly too: OUTCAR `Iteration M(N)` = M-th *electronic* step of the N-th *ionic* step; DIMCAR step = the N-th dimer *translation* ‚Äî the parenthesized `(N)` matches the DIMCAR step.) A start *already* meeting `|EDIFFG|` (CI-NEB at EDIFFG, or any low-force geometry) stops the dimer after one step (IOPT=1/2 both immediately); below `|EDIFFG|` *before* the first rotation ‚áí exits with DIMCAR dashes, **no** negative mode confirmed. Case (d) is the *opposite*: a large OUTCAR force after a one-step halt means the run didn't converge, it **stopped** ‚Äî either `NSW=1` finished it by design, or the executable **died/hung after the first ionic step** (a bad build / MPI link, not the VTST algorithm).
**Check.** `grep 'reached required accuracy' OUTCAR`, then `grep -E 'FORCES: max atom|by dimension' OUTCAR` ‚Äî per-atom max `< |EDIFFG|` ‚áí genuinely converged. Dashes case: compare the DIMCAR `Force` (= initial `|F|`) to `|EDIFFG|`. Never treat the printed/DIMCAR `Force` as the test.
**Fix.** Genuinely converged ‚áí **don't re-run because the printout looks high**; a dashes/one-step exit confirmed no curvature, so verify the saddle by **frequency** (one imaginary mode). If you *intended a search* but it quit immediately, the start already met `|EDIFFG|` ‚Äî EDIFFG **too loose**, started **at/near a minimum**, or the MODECAR (built on the initial structure) points **back toward** it: tighten `EDIFFG`, start from non-negligible forces, verify MODECAR direction (animate ‚Äî MODECAR entry). The same force-only exit (no curvature gate ‚Äî *by design*; no INCAR tag forces a negative mode) can also converge *mid-run* on a **near-zero/weakly-positive zero mode** on a **flat PES** (small barrier, near-degenerate sites), not just exit in a single row ‚Äî same cure: a **significant displacement** off the stable site + a good dimer direction (drift-entry zero-mode note). A genuinely tighter saddle on a real one? *deliberately* lower `EDIFFG` (‚àí0.01/‚àí0.005) and continue with `DRotMax`‚Üí10; if that makes it *wander off*, the start wasn't near a saddle. **Case (d) ‚Äî large OUTCAR force + one-step halt** ‚áí not a saddle but a *stop*: `grep NSW INCAR` (`NSW=1` ends it by design ‚áí raise it); NSW fine ‚áí the binary **died/hung**, so verify the executable's compilation & MPI linkage (reproduce on a known-good build) *before* suspecting any VTST/algorithm bug ‚Äî the cross-cutting mis-compiled-binary reflex (SKILL.md version-first).
**Rule.** Judge by OUTCAR `FORCES: max atom`, never the printed/DIMCAR `Force` (the DIMCAR one is the mode-projected total force at the dimer center ‚Äî a different quantity, expected > `|EDIFFG|` at convergence). Likewise a Lanczos **quench** line printing `g(F)= 0.000E+00 g(S)= 0.000E+00` every ionic step is **normal** ‚Äî Lanczos doesn't drive ionic relaxation through the quench optimizer, so those norms are placeholders, *not* static forces or a stall; judge from the **NEWMODECAR update + curvature**, never the quench norms. A single DIMCAR row with `---` Torque/Curvature/Angle + energy = initial POSCAR = start already below `|EDIFFG|`, exited before rotating (no mode confirmed) ‚Äî fine if handed a converged saddle (confirm by frequency), else EDIFFG too loose / at a minimum / MODECAR mis-aimed. The **mirror** ‚Äî a *large* OUTCAR force after a one-step halt with no crash ‚Äî is a real *stop*, not a false alarm: check `NSW` (=1 ends by design), then the executable/MPI build, before the algorithm. (Expert 2008, 2016, 2018 & 2021, confirmed 2022; force-only exit longstanding, not version-specific. One-step-halt-with-large-forces ‚áí NSW/broken-build OLD 2006 ‚Äî that era's specific architecture list is obsolete, but the symptom‚ÜíNSW-then-build logic stands.)

## Dimer wanders / high force after removing frozen-atom constraints ‚Äî a new, harder search; prefer IOPT=1
**Symptom.** A dimer that converged cleanly *with* frozen-atom constraints (e.g. after a constrained CI-NEB) diverges or wanders once the constraints are lifted. Under the now-free system, `IOPT=1` (LBFGS) reconverges to a low force while `IOPT=2` (CG) does not ‚Äî the geometry drifts as if hunting a different saddle.
**Why.** Lifting `F F F` constraints changes the PES curvature the optimizer sees; reconverging the freed saddle is a *distinct, harder* problem than the original constrained search, not a continuation of it. `IOPT=2` (CG) is more sensitive to the changed landscape and can track toward a different saddle; `IOPT=1` (LBFGS) is more robust here.
**Check.** `grep 'FORCES: max atom' OUTCAR` (not DIMCAR) per optimizer; `grep -E 'IOPT|DRotMax' INCAR`; confirm the POSCAR went from constrained rows to all-`T T T` (the over-freezing caveat lives under selective-dynamics in parameters.md).
**Fix.** Prefer `IOPT=1` for reconverging a saddle after constraint removal. If `IOPT=2` is required, raise `DRotMax` (e.g. to 10) for fuller rotation convergence each step. If it still wanders, suspect the unconstrained system has a *nearby, different* saddle than the constrained one ‚Äî confirm by frequency (one imaginary mode along the intended coordinate).
**Rule.** Reconverging a dimer after lifting frozen-atom constraints is a new search, not a restart: default to `IOPT=1`, give `IOPT=2` a larger `DRotMax`, and read persistent wandering as a different nearby saddle. (Expert-stated 2022; not version-specific.)

## `diminit.pl` single-image dimer = POSCAR (center) + DISPLACECAR_sp (axis) ‚Äî no second image file; pick the init route by available info
**Symptom.** Unsure what `diminit.pl` produces (a midpoint? original-plus-offset?) and which files feed the run; **or** you fed `diminit.pl` a *TS-guess* POSCAR and got meaningless image files; **or** you know the active atoms but have no saddle intuition and don't want to bias the mode toward a guessed TS.
**Pick the init route by what you have.** `diminit.pl` is the **random-exploration-from-a-*minimum*** tool ‚Äî it wants a *minimum* POSCAR + `DISPLACECAR` (which atoms to displace + average magnitude) for the *unknown-mechanism*/adaptive-KMC case; hand it a **TS guess and it returns meaningless structures** (a present `DISPLACECAR` ‚áí you're in the random/KMC workflow ‚Äî the wrong tool once you already know the saddle region). Otherwise route by info: from a **band ‚Üí `neb2dim.pl`** (MODECAR entry); from **IS + a TS guess ‚Üí encode the IS‚ÜíTS vector as the dimer axis** ‚Äî legacy two-image `interpolate.pl IS TS 0.99` & `1.01` (two images straddling the TS along the line back to IS), modern single-image a MODECAR along IS‚ÜíTS; with **only a TS guess** (no reaction-coordinate info) ‚Üí no curvature given ‚áí random direction (the unbiased start below).
**Rule.** A single-image dimer is *fully specified* by two files: `diminit.pl` writes **POSCAR = the dimer center** and the dimer **axis** (`DISPLACECAR_sp`); the opposite endpoint is implicit (center ¬± ¬Ω¬∑step), so no offset POSCAR is needed (`ls DISPLACECAR_sp` confirms). **(Legacy layout ‚Äî don't recreate it.)** The *original* (~2007) dimer ran NEB-style ‚Äî two dirs `01/`,`02/`, one POSCAR (a dimer endpoint) each, shared `INCAR/KPOINTS/POTCAR` in the parent (route-paragraph recipes). Current `ICHAIN=2` builds use this single-image form, so **don't make `01/`,`02/` for a modern dimer** ‚Äî and **Lanczos sets up identically**: one flat dir (like a normal relaxation), POSCAR (saddle guess) + INCAR/KPOINTS/POTCAR, **MODECAR optional** (absent ‚áí random initial mode, present ‚áí it seeds the search) ‚Äî no NEB-style subdirs, no *required* mode; the current lowest mode is (re)written every Lanczos step (`NEWMODECAR`), so a kill/restart reuses the latest mode (copy ‚Üí `MODECAR`); from a converged band seed with `neb2lan.pl` (writes the saddle POSCAR + an optional MODECAR). *(Reading an old two-image run: the converged saddle is **neither** `01/CONTCAR` nor `02/CONTCAR` ‚Äî they straddle it (`DNdR`‚âà0.005 √Ö) ‚Äî but their **midpoint**, `interpolate.pl 01/CONTCAR 02/CONTCAR 0.5`. Modern single-image builds write the saddle estimate to CONTCAR each ionic step ‚Äî read it there, per the CENTCAR/CONTCAR entry above, **not** CENTCAR. Affected legacy VTST pre-v2.0; v2.0 introduced the single-image/CENTCAR form ~2007.)* **Unbiased start (no saddle guess):** give the chosen atoms per-atom **Gaussian œÉ** in `DISPLACECAR_sp` and let `diminit.pl` draw a *random* displacement, setting **MODECAR = min‚Üídisplaced** ‚Äî an unbiased initial mode, not a hand-crafted direction (the single-start form of akmc's batched random-displacement survey, workflows.md; grade by DIMCAR step 1, MODECAR entry). (Expert-stated 2021-06-04 & 2006 ‚Äî dimer-init 11-12, Lanczos flat-dir 05-03; route-selection / `interpolate.pl` 0.99/1.01 / diminit-needs-a-minimum / Lanczos `ICHAIN`/`LLANCZOS` flags from early vtstscripts ‚Äî verify script names/format & tag names in current releases; concepts not version-specific.)

## MODECAR for a dimer ‚Äî derive it from the band with `neb2dim.pl`, then animate to verify direction
**Symptom.** You have IS/FS POSCARs (or NEB images) and want to start a dimer but are unsure how to build the MODECAR ‚Äî *or* you hold a MODECAR but can't tell what displacement it encodes (per-atom vectors aren't human-readable), so you can't confirm the initial mode points along the intended reaction coordinate before launch.
**Build it from the band ‚Äî seed from the *peak* image.** An interpolated NEB image isn't a stationary point, so free-relaxing any but the converged peak follows the gradient to the nearest minimum (usually back to the reactant ‚Äî *expected, not pathological*). So take the band's highest-energy image and, when *any* NEB exists (even rough/loosely-converged), derive the MODECAR with `neb2dim.pl`, not a guessed vector: it reads the band `0?/OUTCAR`s and writes the interpolated-saddle POSCAR *plus* a MODECAR on the reaction-coordinate tangent (handoff in workflows.md). *Below the floor:* a **never-run linear interpolation** (raw `nebmake.pl` output) has no energy variation along the band, so neb2dim finds no maximum and writes the *same* straight I‚ÜíF chord MODECAR for *whatever* image pair you pick ‚Äî no curvature, no saddle direction, and the dimer just relaxes back toward a minimum. Pre-converge the band a few NEB iterations to forces <~1 eV/√Ö *first* so neb2dim can interpolate the MODECAR to the true energy peak. **A *blank/malformed* MODECAR from a vtstscripts mode-gen script (`neb2dim.pl`/`generate_dimer.py`) with *no* error ‚Äî while `modemake.pl` builds one fine on the *same* files (expert can't reproduce it on your inputs; a fresh 'latest' can lag a same-day patch ‚Äî re-fetch/send files) ‚Äî is a *stale vtstscripts*, not bad inputs:** an old script bug since fixed ‚áí `wc -l MODECAR` (empty/short) + check the rev atop the script, then update vtstscripts (latest Henkelman distribution) before debugging files (affected old revs ‚â≤ 835, ~2012; current ones unaffected). **Grade the seed by DIMCAR step 1 ‚Äî two mirror tells.** Force *and* torque dropping monotonically with *negative* curvature from step 1 ‚áí a **good** seed *even from a partially-converged NEB*: the rotation realigns the mode and translation drives it in, self-correcting a rough geometry, so you needn't fully converge the band first ‚Äî only land its peak in the right region. **But a bad *band* poisons the seed:** starting ~1 eV *above* the highest NEB image with *large positive* curvature ‚áí the seed image sat in an **intermediate minimum** the path kinks around, so neb2dim's interpolated tangent there is ill-defined. *Post-hoc*, the same fault reads as a dimer that **converges below *both* NEB endpoints** (apparent negative barrier) with an **unexpected extra bond change** in CONTCAR (positive curvature / no imaginary mode = a minimum, not a saddle) ‚Äî the seed lay on the *downhill slope into* the intermediate and slid in; **not** fixable by bumping `DRotMax`/lowering `DFNMax` (rotation knobs can't pull a seed uphill out of a basin). Check band geometry ‚Äî `nebresults.pl` reporting an extremum *minimum* flanked by two peaks near one image, or the OUTCAR `NEB: distance to prev, next image, angle between` line **<~90¬∞** at an interior image (~180¬∞ on a straight path) ‚Äî then **fix the band**: **subdivide at the intermediate minimum into two sub-bands** (IS‚Üíintermediate, intermediate‚ÜíFS) and dimer the highest image of *each* for the two separate saddles, or rerun with 7‚Äì9 images + climbing ‚Äî not the mode: a manual IS‚ÜíFS `modemake.pl` MODECAR can't repair an under-resolved band.
**Verify direction before launch ‚Äî a wrong-pointing mode drifts or converges to a *different* saddle (drift entry).** MODECAR is a displacement *direction*, not positions (magnitudes fractional ~0‚Äì1 ‚áí direct, larger ‚áí Cartesian). Animate it ‚Äî a 2-image pseudo-trajectory (image 0 = minimum POSCAR, image 1 = minimum + MODECAR scaled to a visible amplitude) in VESTA/ASE/OVITO ‚Äî and watch the motion lie along the intended coordinate. Seeding from a converged CI-NEB, instead run frequency on the CI image: its imaginary mode must point along that coordinate ‚Äî *no* target-bond motion flags the CI image as **not** the TS (a shoulder or another path's saddle, PES-shoulder entry).
**Rule.** Seed a dimer from the band's *peak* image, never by free-relaxing an interpolated one (falls to the nearest minimum, usually the reactant; expected). Build the MODECAR with `neb2dim.pl` (hand-build only with no band); an *empty* one with no error while `modemake.pl` works = **stale vtstscripts ‚Üí update**, not bad inputs. Grade by DIMCAR step 1: force+torque dropping with *negative* curvature = good even from a *partially*-converged NEB; well above the NEB max with *positive* curvature = bad *band* (intermediate min / kinked path) ‚Üí subdivide or add images, don't hand-tune the mode. Verify direction (animate equilibrium‚Üídisplaced, or frequency on a CI image) before launch. (neb2dim-from-band recent; step-1 tells & free-relax-to-min 2012; below-both-endpoints intermediate-min tell & linear-interp-floor OLD 2010; empty-MODECAR stale-script MID 2012, version-caveated ‚Äî all defer to: fix the band, not the mode.)
